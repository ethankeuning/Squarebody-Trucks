Chevy C/K-R/V10 Pickup

The Squarebody pickup, made from 1973 to 1987, is arguably one of the most iconic trucks of all time.

The Squarebody Pickup is rareley kept in it's stock configuration. Most people lift the K/V10, and lover the C/R10.

Another common modification is to LS swap the truck. This means putting a new fuel injeced motor from chevy under the hood, commonly accompanied by a new transmission.

As seen above, there are different types of pickups: C,R,V, and K. These all mean different things:

  • The "C" means non throttle body injected, 2 Wheel drive.
  • The "R" is similar, meaning Throttle Body Injected, 2 Wheel Drive.
  • "V" shows that the truck is Throttle Body Injected, with 4 Wheel Drive.
  • Trucks with a "K" in the name are non Throttle Body Injected are 4 Wheel drive.

Chevy Blazer

Also made from 73'-87' is the Chevy Blazer. This vehicle was made specifically to target the Ford Bronco.

Chevy Suburban

Made in the same years as the other trucks, the Chevy Suburban is an extremely large vehicle designed to carry lots of people and cargo.
